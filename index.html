Grounding Techniques

Use your senses to anchor yourself in the present moment. Try the 5-4-3-2-1 method, or walk barefoot outdoors.

Research: Grounding reduces stress and improves mood (Chevalier et al., 2012).

Proprioceptive Input

Heavy work like stretching, yoga, or compression helps your brain feel safe and calm.

Research: Effective for sensory regulation (Ayres, 1972).

Breathwork

Box breathing or slow diaphragmatic breathing activates the calming parasympathetic system.

Research: Reduces anxiety, improves HRV (Jerath et al., 2006).

Cold Exposure

Cold water or ice on the face activates the vagus nerve and can bring instant calm.

Research: Stimulates the dive reflex (Porges, 2001).

Cognitive Labeling

Simply naming what you feel can reduce its emotional charge. Try “I’m feeling...” to engage your thinking brain.

Research: Decreases amygdala activity (Lieberman et al., 2007).

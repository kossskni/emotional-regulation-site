Evidence-Based Emotional Regulation

Explore science-backed strategies to calm your body and mind.

1. Grounding Techniques

Grounding is a method to bring awareness to the present moment. Techniques include the 5-4-3-2-1 method, walking barefoot, or holding textured objects.

Research: Grounding can reduce cortisol and improve mood (Chevalier et al., 2012).

2. Proprioceptive Input

Proprioception helps calm the nervous system through heavy work like stretching, yoga, or using compression items.

Research: Based on sensory integration theory (Ayres, 1972).

3. Breathwork

Box breathing or diaphragmatic breathing can shift your nervous system to a calmer state.

Research: Improves HRV and reduces anxiety (Jerath et al., 2006).

4. Cold Exposure

Cold water or ice can activate the vagus nerve and lower arousal.

Research: Activates dive reflex (Porges, 2001).

5. Emotion Labeling

Labeling emotions helps regulate them by engaging your prefrontal cortex.

Research: Reduces amygdala activity (Lieberman et al., 2007).

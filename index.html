Regulation Strategies for Young People

Feeling overwhelmed, anxious, angry or disconnected? These strategies can help you find balance and feel more in control. They are grounded in neuroscience and easy to use anytime, anywhere.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Notice 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste.

Box Breathing

Breathe in for 4 seconds, hold for 4, out for 4, hold again for 4. Repeat.

Jump or Move

Short bursts of movement help release tension and regulate energy.

Cold Water Splash

Splash your face or run your wrists under cold water to reset your nervous system.

Name It to Tame It

Label your emotion: 'I'm feeling sad.' This reduces intensity.

Touch Something Textured

Hold a soft or rough object like a rock, fidget toy, or plushie.

Push or Squeeze

Push palms together, hug a pillow, or squeeze a stress ball.

Safe Space Visualization

Imagine a peaceful, safe place in detail. Use all your senses.

Body Scan

Focus attention slowly from toes to head. Notice any tension.

Positive Affirmations

Say: 'I am safe.' 'I can get through this.' 'I’m not alone.'

Music Grounding

Listen to calming or favorite music and really pay attention to it.

Color Search

Pick a color and find as many objects around you with that color.

If you are ever in immediate danger or need urgent help:
Call emergency services (999 in the UK), or talk to a trusted adult.
You can also contact Childline (UK) at 0800 1111 – free and confidential.
You are not alone, and there is always someone who can help.

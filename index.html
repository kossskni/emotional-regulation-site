Emotional Regulation Strategies

Use these science-backed tools to feel calmer, more in control, and better connected to yourself.

Download PDF Checklist

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

Notice 5 things you can see, 4 touch, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste.

Box Breathing

Breathe in 4s, hold 4s, out 4s, hold 4s.

Movement

Stretch, dance, jump to release energy.

Cold Splash

Cold water on face/wrists resets your system.

Name It

Say or write your feelings aloud.

Textured Touch

Hold soft or bumpy items like fidget toys.

Push/Hug

Push hands together or hug a pillow.

Safe Place Visualization

Imagine a calm place in detail.

Body Scan

Focus from toes to head, releasing tension.

Affirmations

Say: I am safe. I am enough.

Music

Listen to music and focus on sounds.

Color Hunt

Look for everything blue (or another color) around you.

Why Practice Matters

These strategies are like muscles — the more you use them, the stronger they get. At first, they might feel awkward or not seem to help right away. But with practice, your brain learns to calm down faster and easier.

Science shows that it can take about 20 to 30 times using a new strategy before it feels natural. Some changes in the brain can happen in just 3 to 4 weeks of regular practice.

So try different ones, use them when you're calm or stressed, and don’t give up. Your brain is learning even when you don’t notice right away!

Safe Space

Body Check

Close your eyes and notice each part of your body from toes to head. Relax as you go.

Positive Self-Talk

Color Focus
